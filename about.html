About

A Jewelry Guide Built for Clearer Browsing

The Jewel Index started from a simple frustration: most jewelry shopping happens quickly through photos, prices, and broad words like elegant, timeless, or statement-making. Those details can be helpful, but they do not always explain what affects fit, comfort, wear, or occasion.

This site exists to make jewelry browsing clearer. Each guide breaks down specific details such as setting types, chain length, pendant drop, wrist and finger sizing, clasp and backing styles, metal types, and plating differences so visitors know what to check before reviewing a retailer page.

Approach

Our Approach

What The Jewel Index Does

The Jewel Index researches jewelry categories, construction details, and styling considerations, then explains them in plain language. The goal is to help visitors compare rings, necklaces, bracelets, earrings, gift ideas, and style notes with more confidence.

What The Jewel Index Does Not Do

The Jewel Index does not sell jewelry directly, process orders, handle payments, ship items, or manage returns. It also does not claim to personally test, purchase, wear, or verify every piece unless clearly stated.

How To Use This Site

Use The Jewel Index before comparing retailer listings. Retailer pages are the source of truth for current pricing, availability, materials, shipping timelines, sizing charts, and return policies. The guides help visitors know what questions to ask when they get there.
